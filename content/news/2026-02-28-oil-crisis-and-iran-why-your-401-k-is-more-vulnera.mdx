The Strait of Hormuz is back in the headlines. With U.S.-Iran tensions escalating, oil markets are pricing in the possibility of a major supply disruption at the world's most critical energy chokepoint. According to the Energy Information Administration, approximately 21 million barrels of oil flow through the strait daily -- roughly 21% of global petroleum consumption.

Iran itself produces about 3.2 million barrels per day. If military strikes or blockades take even a fraction of that offline, the price impact would be immediate and severe. Yahoo Finance reports that traders are already building risk premiums into crude prices ahead of any actual disruption.

## The Strait of Hormuz: By the Numbers

| Metric | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Daily oil flow through Strait | ~21 million barrels | EIA |
| Share of global oil supply | ~21% | EIA |
| Iran's daily production | 3.2 million barrels | OPEC Monthly Report |
| Saudi Arabia's daily exports via Strait | 6.3 million barrels | EIA |
| Global spare production capacity | ~3.5 million barrels/day | IEA |
| U.S. Strategic Petroleum Reserve | 372 million barrels (as of Jan. 2025) | DOE |

The math is straightforward. If 5-10 million barrels per day are disrupted and global spare capacity is only 3.5 million barrels, the gap cannot be filled. Prices spike. The U.S. Strategic Petroleum Reserve, at its lowest level since 1984, provides limited cushion.

## How Oil Shocks Have Historically Hit Retirement Portfolios

Every major oil supply disruption in the past 50 years has followed a recognizable pattern for financial markets:

1. Oil prices spike
2. Transportation and manufacturing costs rise within weeks
3. Consumer prices (food, goods, services) follow within 2-4 months
4. Corporate profit margins get squeezed
5. Stock prices fall as earnings decline
6. The Federal Reserve faces a dilemma: raise rates (hurting stocks further) or hold steady (allowing inflation to persist)
7. Bonds lose value in real terms regardless of the Fed's choice

Here are the actual numbers from past disruptions:

| Crisis | Peak Oil Price | S&P 500 (Next 12 Mo.) | Gold (Next 12 Mo.) | Inflation Peak |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1973 Arab embargo | $11.65/bbl (+300%) | -17.4% | +73% | 12.3% |
| 1979 Iran revolution | $39.50/bbl (+150%) | +1.4% | +120% | 14.8% |
| 1990 Gulf War | $41/bbl (+90%) | -3.1% | +5% | 6.1% |
| 2008 supply spike | $147/bbl (+94%) | -38.5% | +5.5% | 5.6% |

*Sources: Federal Reserve Economic Data, S&P Global, World Gold Council, BLS*

In four out of four major oil shocks, gold outperformed the S&P 500 in the following year. In three out of four, gold delivered double-digit positive returns while stocks fell.

## What a $100+ Oil Scenario Means for Your 401(k)

The average American 401(k) balance for workers aged 55-64 is $207,874 (Vanguard How America Saves 2024). Most of that money sits in a combination of stock index funds and bond funds.

Here is a scenario analysis if oil moves to $100-$120 per barrel:

| Portfolio Element | Likely Impact | Estimated Loss on $200,000 |
|---|---|---|
| U.S. stock funds (60%) | -10% to -20% (earnings compression) | -$12,000 to -$24,000 |
| Bond funds (30%) | -3% to -8% (inflation fears, rate pressure) | -$1,800 to -$4,800 |
| Cash/money market (10%) | 0% nominal, -4% to -6% real | -$800 to -$1,200 real loss |
| **Total portfolio impact** | | **-$14,600 to -$30,000** |

That is the nominal hit. Add inflation running at 5-7% (consistent with past oil shocks), and the purchasing power loss is even steeper.

## Why the Federal Reserve Cannot Fix This

The Fed's toolkit is designed for demand-driven problems. Oil shocks are supply-driven.

- **If the Fed raises rates** to fight inflation, it crushes an already-weakened economy and hammers stock prices further
- **If the Fed holds rates steady**, inflation runs hotter and erodes purchasing power faster
- **If the Fed cuts rates** to support markets, it risks fueling the inflation it is supposed to fight

During the 2022 inflation episode, the Fed chose aggressive rate hikes. The result: the worst year for bonds since 1842 (Bloomberg Aggregate Bond Index fell 13%) while stocks also fell. Retirees holding a standard 60/40 portfolio had nowhere to hide.

Gold finished 2022 essentially flat at -0.3%, then surged to new all-time highs in 2023 and 2024 as the dollar weakened.

## Practical Diversification Steps

1. **Check your actual allocation.** Log into your 401(k) and see what percentage is in stocks vs. bonds vs. cash. If it is all paper assets, you are fully exposed to this scenario.
2. **Ask about in-service rollovers.** If you are 55 or older, many 401(k) plans allow you to roll a portion into an IRA while still working. This opens up asset classes not available in your 401(k).
3. **Consider a 5-15% allocation to physical gold.** A self-directed IRA allows you to hold IRS-approved gold and silver bars and coins with the same tax treatment as your current retirement account.
4. **Keep contributing enough to capture employer match.** The match is free money -- do not give that up. But any contributions above the match threshold could go into a more diversified vehicle.
