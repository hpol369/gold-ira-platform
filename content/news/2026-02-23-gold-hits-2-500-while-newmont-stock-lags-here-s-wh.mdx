Gold crossed the $2,500 mark for the first time, finishing 2024 up roughly 27% according to the World Gold Council. Newmont Corporation, the world's largest gold miner by market cap, returned about 35% in the same year after a rough 2023. But that headline number hides a pattern worth understanding: gold mining stocks and physical gold don't move in lockstep, and the gap matters if you're building a retirement portfolio.

## Gold vs. Gold Miners: The Performance Gap

Over the last 20 years, physical gold and gold mining stocks have told very different stories:

| Period | Gold (spot price) | GDX (Gold Miners ETF) |
|---|---|---|
| 2008 (financial crisis) | +5.5% | -26.8% |
| 2013 (taper tantrum) | -28.3% | -54.5% |
| 2020 (COVID crash, March) | -1.6% | -38.2% |
| 2024 (full year) | +27.2% | +10.6% |
| 20-year annualized return | +8.4% | +2.1% |

Data sources: World Gold Council, VanEck (GDX issuer), Bloomberg.

The pattern is consistent. When markets panic, mining stocks drop alongside other equities. Physical gold tends to hold steady or rise. That's the difference between owning a commodity and owning a company that produces it.

## Why Mining Stocks Add Layers of Risk

Newmont's stock price depends on far more than the gold price:

- **Operating costs.** Newmont reported all-in sustaining costs (AISC) of $1,444 per ounce in Q3 2024, up from $1,215 a year earlier. Rising diesel, labor, and equipment costs squeeze margins even when gold prices rise.
- **Production challenges.** The company's 2024 production guidance was 6.9 million ounces, but it lowered expectations twice during the year due to operational issues at its Lihir mine in Papua New Guinea.
- **Debt and dilution.** Newmont carries about $8 billion in long-term debt following its 2023 Newcrest acquisition. That debt gets serviced regardless of gold prices.
- **Management decisions.** In late 2024, Newmont announced plans to divest up to $2 billion in non-core assets. Corporate restructuring adds uncertainty that physical gold never has.
- **Geopolitical exposure.** Newmont operates mines in 8 countries including Ghana, Peru, and Australia. Regulatory changes, tax increases, or political instability in any of these locations affects the stock, not the gold price.

A bar of gold sitting in a vault has none of these risks.

## What Central Banks Are Actually Buying

Central banks aren't loading up on mining stocks. They're buying physical metal.

According to the World Gold Council's 2024 Gold Demand Trends report:

- Central banks purchased **1,037 tonnes** of gold in 2023, the second-highest annual total ever recorded
- **China's** People's Bank of China added gold for 18 consecutive months through late 2024
- **Poland** repatriated 100 tonnes of gold from the Bank of England to Warsaw
- **India** added 37 tonnes to reserves in the first three quarters of 2024
- Central bank gold holdings globally now exceed **36,000 tonnes**

These institutions employ thousands of analysts. They chose physical gold, not shares in Newmont or Barrick. That tells you something about how serious money managers view the distinction.

## The Dollar Side of the Equation

Gold's rise to $2,500 is partly a gold story, but it's also a dollar story. The Federal Reserve's balance sheet expanded from $4.2 trillion in early 2020 to a peak of $8.9 trillion in April 2022. Even after quantitative tightening, it sat at roughly $6.8 trillion at the end of 2024.

The BLS Consumer Price Index shows the dollar lost about **22.7% of its purchasing power** between January 2020 and December 2024. Priced in 2020 dollars, gold at $2,500 isn't as expensive as the headline suggests. It reflects the currency losing value as much as the metal gaining it.

The U.S. national debt crossed **$36 trillion** in January 2025 (Treasury Department). Interest payments on that debt exceeded $1 trillion in fiscal year 2024, surpassing defense spending for the first time. That trajectory puts continued pressure on the dollar's purchasing power.

## How Physical Gold Fits in a Retirement Account

IRS rules allow physical gold in retirement accounts through a self-directed IRA, provided it meets fineness standards (99.5% purity for gold, per IRC Section 408(m)(3)(B)). Eligible forms include:

- American Gold Eagle coins (1 oz, 1/2 oz, 1/4 oz, 1/10 oz)
- American Gold Buffalo coins
- Canadian Gold Maple Leaf coins
- Gold bars from NYMEX or COMEX-approved refiners

The metal must be held by an IRS-approved depository, not your home safe. Rollovers from existing 401(k)s or traditional IRAs can be done as direct trustee-to-trustee transfers without triggering taxes or penalties.

This isn't about going all-in on gold. It's about whether a portion of your retirement savings should be in an asset that doesn't depend on corporate earnings reports, management decisions, or stock market sentiment.
