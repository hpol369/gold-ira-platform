President Trump's aggressive stance toward Iran is creating ripple effects that could hit American retirees where it hurts most - their wallets. 

Despite campaigning on affordability promises, escalating tensions with Iran threaten to drive up prices on everything from gas to groceries. **Iran controls about 10% of global oil exports**, and any disruption to Middle Eastern supply chains sends energy costs soaring. When energy gets expensive, everything else follows - food, transportation, manufacturing, you name it.

## What This Means for Your Retirement

If you're sitting on a traditional retirement portfolio of stocks and bonds, you're about to get schooled in real-world economics. **Rising geopolitical tensions typically mean market volatility and inflationary pressure** - a double hit to retirement security.

Your fixed-income investments get crushed when inflation rises. That "safe" bond portfolio paying 3%? It's actually losing money if inflation runs 4-5%. Your 401(k) might look stable on paper, but **if gas hits $5 per gallon and groceries cost 20% more, your retirement dollars buy less every month**.

Here's the kicker: Social Security and pensions don't adjust fast enough to keep up with rapid price increases. You could be looking at months or years of declining purchasing power while bureaucrats debate cost-of-living adjustments.
