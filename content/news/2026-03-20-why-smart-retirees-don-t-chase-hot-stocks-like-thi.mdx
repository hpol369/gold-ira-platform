## What This Means for Your Retirement

If you're 55 or older with a traditional 401(k) or IRA, ask yourself this: **How much of your retirement is tied to the stock market's mood swings?**

Let's say you have $500,000 in retirement savings. If 80% of that is in stocks and the market corrects 30% (which happens regularly), you just lost $120,000. At your age, do you have time to wait 5-7 years for it to come back?

Here's the bigger problem: even if that aerospace stock doubles, you're still holding dollars that are losing purchasing power every single day. Your grocery bill knows this. Your utility bills know this. Only your financial advisor pretends inflation isn't real.
