Ship traffic through the Strait of Hormuz has plummeted to roughly 8 vessels per day -- a 94% drop from normal levels, according to MarketWatch shipping data. This 21-mile-wide waterway between Iran and Oman normally handles about 20.5 million barrels of oil per day, representing roughly 21% of global petroleum consumption, per the U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA).

The disruption is already rippling through energy markets. Brent crude jumped above $87 per barrel in early March 2026, up from $76 just six weeks earlier. But the impact on retirement accounts goes well beyond gas prices.

## Why Oil Chokepoints Hit Retirement Portfolios

The Strait of Hormuz is one of only a handful of maritime bottlenecks that keep the global economy running. When it constricts, inflation pressures build across every sector -- not just energy.

Here is how previous oil supply disruptions affected U.S. markets, according to Federal Reserve Bank of Dallas research:

| Crisis | Oil Price Spike | S&P 500 Impact | CPI Inflation Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1973 Arab oil embargo | +300% | -48% over 20 months | +8.7 percentage points |
| 1979 Iranian Revolution | +150% | -17% over 18 months | +6.3 percentage points |
| 1990 Gulf War | +93% | -20% over 3 months | +1.9 percentage points |
| 2022 Russia-Ukraine | +65% | -25% over 9 months | +3.5 percentage points |

The pattern is consistent: oil supply shocks trigger both rising inflation and falling stock prices simultaneously. That is the worst possible combination for someone drawing income from a retirement portfolio.

## The Dual Threat to Your 401(k)

When oil prices spike, two things happen to a standard stock-and-bond retirement portfolio:

1. **Stock prices fall.** Higher energy costs squeeze corporate profit margins. During the 2022 Russia-Ukraine shock, the S&P 500 lost 25.4% from its January peak to its October trough, according to S&P Global data.

2. **Inflation erodes purchasing power.** The Bureau of Labor Statistics reported CPI inflation hit 9.1% in June 2022 -- the highest since 1981. That means retirees drawing $4,000 per month effectively lost $364 per month in real purchasing power within a year.

For a retiree with $500,000 in a 60/40 stock-bond portfolio, the 2022 experience looked like this:

- **Portfolio decline:** Approximately -16.9% (S&P 500 -18.1%, Bloomberg Agg Bond -13.0%)
- **Dollar value lost:** ~$84,500
- **Inflation-adjusted loss:** ~$129,500 when factoring in 8%+ real price increases

That is over a quarter of the portfolio's purchasing power gone in 12 months.

## Gold During Oil Crises: The Historical Record

Gold has a documented track record during oil supply disruptions. According to World Gold Council data:

- **1973-1974 oil embargo:** Gold rose 66.1%
- **1979-1980 Iranian Revolution:** Gold rose 120.4%
- **1990 Gulf War:** Gold rose 8.7% during the initial invasion period
- **2022 Russia-Ukraine:** Gold rose 0.4% for the year, outperforming both stocks (-18.1%) and bonds (-13.0%)

The reason is straightforward. Oil supply shocks trigger inflation fears and geopolitical uncertainty -- both conditions where investors historically move capital into physical gold as a store of value.

## The Current Risk Assessment

The EIA identifies the Strait of Hormuz as the world's most important oil transit chokepoint. Countries dependent on Hormuz transit include Saudi Arabia, Iraq, Kuwait, UAE, and Qatar -- collectively representing about 30% of global oil trade.

A prolonged closure or severe restriction of the Strait would likely:

- Push oil above $100 per barrel (Goldman Sachs scenario analysis)
- Add 2-4 percentage points to U.S. inflation (Federal Reserve Bank of Dallas estimates)
- Trigger a 15-25% correction in U.S. equities based on historical precedent

For retirees or near-retirees, the question is not whether another supply shock will happen. The question is whether your portfolio is structured to absorb one.

## Practical Steps

1. **Check your energy sector exposure.** Many S&P 500 index funds are underweight energy and overweight technology -- a vulnerability during oil shocks.
2. **Consider hard asset allocation.** A 5-15% allocation to physical gold within a self-directed IRA provides a hedge specifically calibrated for the type of crisis currently unfolding.
3. **Review your withdrawal strategy.** If you are drawing income, ensure you have 12-24 months of expenses in cash or short-term Treasuries so you are not forced to sell during a downturn.
