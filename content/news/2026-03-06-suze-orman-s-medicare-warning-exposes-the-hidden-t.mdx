Financial expert Suze Orman recently flagged a Medicare cost trap that catches many retirees off guard: Income-Related Monthly Adjustment Amounts (IRMAA) can add thousands of dollars per year to your Medicare premiums, and those charges are deducted directly from your Social Security check before you ever see the money.

The numbers are significant. For 2025, standard Medicare Part B premiums are $185 per month. But IRMAA surcharges can push that to $591.90 per month for higher earners -- an extra $4,883 per year taken straight from Social Security, according to the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS).

## How IRMAA Works

IRMAA uses your Modified Adjusted Gross Income (MAGI) from two years prior. So your 2025 premiums are based on your 2023 tax return. This two-year lookback creates a trap for people who had high earnings in their final working years but have since retired to a lower income.

Here are the 2025 IRMAA brackets, per CMS:

| Individual MAGI | Couple MAGI | Monthly Part B Premium | Monthly Part D Surcharge |
|---|---|---|---|
| $103,000 or less | $206,000 or less | $185.00 | $0 |
| $103,001-$129,000 | $206,001-$258,000 | $259.00 | $13.70 |
| $129,001-$161,000 | $258,001-$322,000 | $370.00 | $35.50 |
| $161,001-$193,000 | $322,001-$386,000 | $480.90 | $57.30 |
| $193,001-$500,000 | $386,001-$750,000 | $591.90 | $79.00 |
| Above $500,000 | Above $750,000 | $628.90 | $85.80 |

A couple earning $260,000 in their last working year pays $518 per month in combined Part B premiums ($259 each) instead of $370. That is an extra $1,776 per year -- money deducted from Social Security payments automatically.

## The Real-Dollar Impact on Social Security

The Social Security Administration reports the average retired worker benefit at $1,976 per month. Here is what retirees actually receive after Medicare deductions at various income levels:

| Scenario | Gross SS Benefit | Part B Premium | Part D Surcharge | Net Monthly Check |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Below IRMAA threshold | $1,976 | -$185 | $0 | $1,791 |
| First IRMAA tier | $1,976 | -$259 | -$13.70 | $1,703 |
| Second IRMAA tier | $1,976 | -$370 | -$35.50 | $1,571 |
| Third IRMAA tier | $1,976 | -$480.90 | -$57.30 | $1,438 |

That top row already shows a 9.4% reduction in take-home Social Security. At the third IRMAA tier, the effective cut reaches 27.2%.

## Medicare Costs Have Outpaced COLA Adjustments

According to Kaiser Family Foundation data, Medicare Part B premiums have increased 208% since 2000, rising from $45.50 to $185 per month. During that same period, Social Security COLA adjustments totaled approximately 82%.

The gap means each year, Medicare takes a larger bite of the Social Security check. The Congressional Budget Office projects Medicare spending will grow from 3.2% of GDP in 2024 to 4.7% of GDP by 2034, driven by an aging population and rising healthcare costs.

Key healthcare cost data for retirees:

- **Average 65-year-old couple's lifetime healthcare costs:** $365,000, per Fidelity's 2025 estimate
- **Average annual out-of-pocket healthcare spending for Medicare beneficiaries:** $7,000, per Kaiser Family Foundation
- **Long-term care (not covered by Medicare):** Median cost of $116,800 per year for a private nursing home room, per Genworth's Cost of Care Survey

## Strategies to Reduce the IRMAA Hit

There are legitimate ways to manage IRMAA exposure:

1. **File an IRMAA appeal (SSA-44 form)** if you experienced a life-changing event like retirement, divorce, or death of a spouse that reduced your income below the threshold used in the two-year lookback.
2. **Manage Roth conversions carefully.** Converting traditional IRA funds to Roth increases MAGI in the conversion year, potentially triggering IRMAA two years later.
3. **Time large capital gains.** Selling appreciated assets in a single year can push you into a higher IRMAA bracket.
4. **Consider tax-free income sources.** Roth IRA withdrawals and municipal bond interest do not count toward MAGI for IRMAA purposes. Physical gold in a Roth IRA, for example, could provide future distributions that do not trigger IRMAA surcharges.

## Building Income That Medicare Cannot Touch

The broader lesson from Orman's warning is that retirement income planning needs to account for deductions most people do not see coming. Between IRMAA, standard Medicare premiums, and rising healthcare costs, your gross Social Security benefit and your net spendable income can differ by 20-30%.
